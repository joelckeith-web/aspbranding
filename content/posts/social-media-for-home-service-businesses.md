Social media for home service businesses works differently than for DTC brands or consumer apps. Nobody scrolls Instagram looking for an HVAC company. They scroll Instagram, see your work on a neighbor's house, remember the name, and call you six months later when their AC dies. Social is a memory and trust layer — not a direct lead driver.

Here's what actually moves revenue for operators, and what's just noise.

## What works

### Consistent proof of work

Before-and-after photos, jobsite videos, tech introductions, completion shots. The content that says "we actually do this, and we're good at it." Weekly at minimum.

### Review amplification

Turn every 5-star Google review into a social post. You're not trying to convert strangers — you're reminding past customers and nearby neighbors that you exist and you're trusted.

### Google Business Profile posts

Often overlooked. GBP posts show up directly in the local search results. A steady cadence of GBP posts is more valuable than Instagram engagement for most home service operators.

### Paid social (Meta) for retargeting

Website visitors who didn't book — put them in a Meta retargeting audience for 30–60 days. Low-budget, high-intent, compounds with other marketing.

## What doesn't work

- Paid Instagram campaigns to cold audiences hoping to drive service calls.
- "Engagement" metrics — likes and follower counts don't book jobs.
- Generic stock-photo branded graphics that look like every other HVAC company's feed.
- TikTok for most trades (some exceptions — pressure washing, landscaping, restoration).

## Common questions

**How many posts per week?** 3–5 per platform, minimum. Less than that and the algorithm forgets you exist.

**Should I be on TikTok?** Only if your trade is visually compelling (pressure washing, landscaping, restoration, dance, fitness) and you'll commit to it.

**What about LinkedIn?** For commercial service divisions — yes. For residential HVAC/plumbing — usually not.
