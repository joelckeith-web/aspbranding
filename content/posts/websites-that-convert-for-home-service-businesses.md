Your website has one job: turn a visitor into a booked appointment. Nothing else matters. Not the award-winning design, not the custom animation, not the "about us" video your cousin shot. If the site doesn't convert, every other marketing dollar you spend is wasted on the way in.

Here's what separates a home service website that books jobs from one that just exists.

## The five things that actually drive conversion

### 1. Page speed

Under 2 seconds on mobile. If the site takes 4 seconds to load, half your paid media budget is lost before the page finishes rendering. This is non-negotiable — and most WordPress sites fail it.

### 2. Trust signals above the fold

Phone number, reviews count + star rating, service area, licenses/certifications, years in business. All visible without scrolling. A visitor should know in 3 seconds: who you are, where you serve, and that you're credible.

### 3. Call tracking on every CTA

If you can't tell which marketing channel drove each phone call, you're flying blind. Dynamic number insertion + call tracking tied to your CRM is table stakes.

### 4. Service area pages + schema

One page per major city you serve, with local schema markup, embedded Google Map, and real photos from that market. This is how one site ranks in 10 metros.

### 5. Mobile-first everything

Sticky call button on mobile. Click-to-call. One-thumb form completion. Over 70% of home service traffic is mobile — design for that, desktop second.
